(SOCIAL) ANXIETY

Anxiety disorders, in a nutshell make those who are affected feel excessively anxious and worried, even in calm situations and with no real reasoning. These people can often feel restless and endangered, and can experience trouble sleeping, hyperventilation, trembling, and increased heart rate.

But what I’ll talk more about, is social anxiety. When a person suffers from social anxiety, they feel extremely overwhelmed and worried about social situations (such as parties, meetings, or even school/work environments), they are overly self-conscious and constantly feel judged, they’re highly scared of being ridiculed around other people. Speaking in public, presenting in front of other people, job interviews, and even answering questions in class can be a massive trigger for them, and it can cause panic attacks. Some of those who have social anxiety might not be triggered by social events, but by performances such as a sport competition or a music performance
