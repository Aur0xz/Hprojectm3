ANOREXIA NERVOSA

What is anorexia?

Anorexia nervosa, usually just called anorexia or ana, is characterized by an intense fear of gaining weight, combined with body dysmorphia. The ones affected by this disorder base their self-worth on their weight and shape; to prevent weight gain, anorexic people severely restrict their caloric intake, and some use also laxatives and diet pills. Treatment and recovery are initially refused by anorexic people, because they don’t feel sick enough, they don’t feel valid, or don’t want to get better as ana tricks them into thinking that it is a good thing for themselves.
